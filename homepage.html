Reachability Predicates in Separation Logic

We study the complexity and expressiveness of separation logics featuring reachability predicates. Separation logic is a mathematical formalism for reasoning about pointer programs. Among the various features of this logic, reachability predicates have a special status, as they allow to express crucial properties of the memory such as acyclicity and garbage-freedom.

Papers:

  • The effects of adding reachability predicates in propositional separation logic (FOSSACS'18)
  • Extending propositional separation logic for robustness properties (FSTTCS'18)
  • An auxiliary logic on trees: on the tower-hardness of logics featuring reachability and submodel reasoning (FOSSACS'20)

Modal Logics with Composition Operators

We develop a framework of modal logics featuring different forms of frame composition that are heavily inspired from separation logics and ambient logics. By relying on standard techniques from finite model theory, we study the expressiveness and computational complexity of our logics, and compare them with more standard formalisms (e.g. graded modal logic).

Papers:

  • Modal Logics with Composition on Finite Forests: Expressivity and Complexity (LICS'20, )
  • A Framework for Reasoning about Dynamic Axioms in Description Logics (IJCAI'20)

Internal Calculi for Spatial Logics

We tackle the open problem of designing internal axiomatisations for spatial logics such as separation logics and ambient logics. Designing an internal (a.k.a. Hilbert-style) axiomatisation for your favourite logic is usually quite challenging. It does not lead necessarily to optimal decision procedures, but the completeness proof usually provides essential insights to better understand the logic at hand.

Papers:

  • Axiomatising Logics with Separating Conjunction and Modalities (JELIA'19)
  • Internal Calculi for Separation Logics (CSL'20)
